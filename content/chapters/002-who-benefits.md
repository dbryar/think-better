Incentives do not explain everything, but they explain a lot. People and institutions often act to protect money, power, attention, votes, status, jobs, brands, relationships, or legal exposure.

That does not require a secret meeting. It often requires only ordinary pressure.

## Common Incentives

- Money: Who profits if this belief spreads?
- Power: Who gains control, authority, or leverage?
- Attention: Who needs clicks, ratings, outrage, or followers?
- Votes: Who benefits from fear, loyalty, or confusion?
- Status: Who becomes important by being believed?
- Survival: Which institution avoids blame or reform?
- Career risk: Who is punished for saying the obvious thing?

## Incentives Are Clues

An incentive is not proof that someone is lying. It is a reason to check carefully.

## Look For Missing Incentives

Sometimes the most useful question is not "who benefits?" but "who is being left out because they are less convenient to blame?"
