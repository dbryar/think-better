## Example 1: "Build The Wall"

Donald Trump's 2015-2016 border-wall promise worked because it turned a complicated policy field into a chant, a physical object, and a named out-group. It paired a visible symbol with a payment claim: the United States would build a wall and Mexico would pay for it.

That does not mean border management is imaginary. It means the message asked voters to treat one object as proof that a leader could restore control.

| Check | What To Ask |
| --- | --- |
| Emotional hook | Does the message make fear feel local, urgent, and solvable by one hard gesture? |
| Target | Are voters being invited to feel mocked, invaded, ignored, or betrayed? |
| Omitted context | What else affects border crossings: labour demand, asylum law, visa overstays, regional instability, enforcement budgets, courts, and employer incentives? |
| Evidence | Does the proposed fix match the actual route, cause, and scale of the problem? |
| Incentive | Who gains votes, airtime, donations, contracts, or attention from keeping the symbol alive? |

Useful source trail:

- The White House archive shows the administration later presenting wall construction as a central achievement: [Remarks by President Trump at the 45th Mile of New Border Wall](https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/briefings-statements/remarks-president-trump-45th-mile-new-border-wall-reynosa-mcallen-tx/).
- The Congressional Research Service backgrounded U.S.-Mexico border barrier funding and authorities: [CRS, DHS Border Barrier Funding](https://www.everycrsreport.com/files/2021-04-07_IN11193_b7e11b086691cf3c02e217b8725d5453dd04135a.html).
- TIME documented the gap between campaign claims that Mexico would pay and later arguments that the payment would be indirect: [Trump Campaigned on Mexico Paying for the Wall](https://time.com/5499391/donald-trump-border-wall-mexico-pay/).

## Example 2: ICE And "Zero Tolerance"

The U.S. 2018 "zero tolerance" period is a strong example of language doing moral work. The phrase sounds neutral and administrative. In practice, the policy context included criminal prosecution of border crossers and family separations that agencies were not prepared to track or repair.

| Phrase | What It Hides |
| --- | --- |
| "Zero tolerance" | A discretionary enforcement choice presented as inevitability. |
| "Law and order" | The need to ask which law, which procedure, which due-process protections, and which human costs. |
| "Deterrence" | A prediction about future behaviour that needs evidence, not just moral force. |
| "Keeping families safe" | A claim that must be checked against what actually happened to children and caregivers. |

Useful source trail:

- The Department of Justice Inspector General reported on the policy's implementation and prosecution decisions: [DOJ OIG, Review of the Department's Planning and Implementation of Its Zero Tolerance Policy](https://oig.justice.gov/sites/default/files/2021-01/2021-01-14.pdf).
- The Department of Homeland Security Inspector General found serious tracking and reunification problems: [DHS OIG, Special Review - Initial Observations Regarding Family Separation Issues](https://www.oig.dhs.gov/sites/default/files/assets/2018-10/OIG-18-84-Sep18.pdf).
- A later DHS OIG report found the department did not have the technology needed to reliably track separated families: [DHS OIG, DHS Lacked Technology Needed to Successfully Account for Separated Migrant Families](https://www.oig.dhs.gov/sites/default/files/assets/2019-11/OIG-20-06-Nov19.pdf).

## Method

Do not stop at "this side is lying." A better breakdown asks whether the message's strongest emotional claim survives contact with details.

| Claim | Better Question |
| --- | --- |
| "Only I can fix it." | What institutions, budgets, laws, and trade-offs would actually be involved? |
| "They are destroying us." | Who exactly is "they", and do they have the power being assigned to them? |
| "The experts are hiding the truth." | Which experts, accountable to whom, and what evidence would prove the claim wrong? |
| "The details are excuses." | Are the details actually where the costs, rights, and implementation failures live? |

## Counterargument

The point is not that every border-control or public-safety argument is bad. The point is that a useful citizen can separate the real policy question from the sales machinery wrapped around it.
