## Fictional Ad

"Your street is less safe because Candidate Vale voted against the Safety Now Bill. Tell Vale to stop putting criminals first."

## Emotional Hook

The ad starts with fear and localises it: "your street." That makes the claim feel immediate before evidence appears.

## Target

The target is a voter who is worried about safety and wants a simple cause.

## Omitted Context

The ad does not explain what was in the bill, why Vale voted against it, whether crime changed, or whether the bill would have improved safety.

## Evidence

The only evidence is a vote. A vote can matter, but it does not prove the motive claimed by the ad.

## Incentive

The sponsor benefits if voters associate Vale with danger.

## Counterargument

Vale may still be wrong. The point is that the ad has not shown enough evidence to prove its strongest claim.
