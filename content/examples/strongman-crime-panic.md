## Pattern

Crime is real. Terrorism is real. Drugs harm people. Public order matters.

That is exactly why strongman messaging works. It starts with a real fear, then tells citizens that courts, journalists, lawyers, opposition parties, civil society, and human-rights limits are luxuries that only protect enemies.

| Claim | Civic Question |
| --- | --- |
| "Only force works." | What evidence shows the policy reduces harm without creating new victims or corruption? |
| "Due process protects criminals." | Who decides who is a criminal before evidence is tested? |
| "Critics support chaos." | Are critics defending crime, or defending rules that also protect innocent people? |
| "Emergency powers are temporary." | What exact date, metric, court, or legislature can end them? |

## Philippines: Duterte's Drug War

Rodrigo Duterte's drug-war politics turned fear of crime and drugs into permission for extreme state violence. The useful lesson is not that public safety should be ignored. It is that "war" language makes it easier to treat poor and marginalised people as disposable proof of action.

Useful source trail:

- Human Rights Watch documented police killings and alleged evidence-faking in Duterte's drug war: [License to Kill](https://www.hrw.org/report/2017/03/02/license-kill/philippine-police-killings-dutertes-war-drugs).
- Amnesty International summarised the campaign's impact on poor and marginalised communities: [The Philippines "War on Drugs"](https://www.amnesty.org/en/projects/the-philippines-war-on-drugs/).
- Stanford's human rights programme summarised Duterte's promise to "solve drugs, criminality, and corruption" through violent measures: [The Current State of Human Rights in the Philippines Under President Duterte](https://humanrights.stanford.edu/events/current-state-human-rights-philippines-under-president-duterte).

## Turkey: Corruption, Media Pressure, And Opposition Control

Turkey shows a different route: elected leadership, then slow institutional tightening. Corruption allegations, media pressure, imprisonment of critics, constitutional changes, and opposition pressure can become one system.

Useful source trail:

- Freedom House rates Turkey "Not Free" and describes the consolidation of power through constitutional change and imprisonment of opponents and critics: [Turkey: Country Profile](https://freedomhouse.org/country/turkey).
- Freedom House's report on corruption, media, and power in Turkey tracks how media pressure and corruption politics interacted: [Democracy in Crisis: Corruption, Media, and Power in Turkey](https://freedomhouse.org/sites/default/files/2020-02/SR_Corruption_Media_Power_Turkey_PDF.pdf).
- Freedom House's 2025 country report notes pressure on journalists and political opponents: [Turkey: Freedom in the World 2025](https://freedomhouse.org/country/turkey/freedom-world/2025).

## The Check

Strongman crime panic is not disproved by saying "crime is fake." That is lazy and often wrong. The stronger test is:

- Is enforcement aimed upward as well as downward?
- Are politically connected people investigated with the same energy as powerless people?
- Can journalists safely inspect police, prisons, courts, contracts, and evidence?
- Do emergency powers expire, or do they become the new constitution?
- Are people safer, or just more afraid of both criminals and the state?

## Better Habit

Never let public safety become a blank cheque. A state powerful enough to ignore rights for your enemies is powerful enough to ignore rights for you.
