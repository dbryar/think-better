## Scenario

A group called Local Families For Fair Choice appears online. It claims to be a spontaneous neighbourhood movement against a new tenant protection law.

## Signals To Check

- Funding is unclear.
- Several posts use identical wording.
- Local photos are stock images or reused across cities.
- Media appearances are arranged before local meetings exist.
- The group avoids naming landlords, developers, or lobby firms.

## Boring Explanation

The campaign may be coordinated by people with money at stake. That does not automatically make every supporter fake, but it changes how you read the message.

## Better Questions

Who paid for the website, ads, polling, and media training? Which local people are actually represented?
