## Pattern

A material issue is hard: housing, wages, debt, corruption, services, climate cost, energy prices, ageing infrastructure, or tax. Then the loudest public argument becomes identity, disgust, and loyalty. The bait may involve immigrants, Muslims, trans people, Roma communities, Jews, refugees, "inner-city elites," students, environmentalists, or rural voters.

The trick is not that culture never matters. Culture matters deeply. The trick is using culture to stop people asking who owns the assets, who wrote the rules, who gets the contract, who avoids scrutiny, and who pays the bill.

## Example: One Nation And Minority Panic In Australia

Pauline Hanson's 1996 parliamentary language about Australia being "swamped by Asians" and her 2016 Senate comeback warning about Muslims are clear examples of political attacks on minorities being framed as national rescue. ABC covered both moments and the continuity between them.

| Move | Check |
| --- | --- |
| Name a minority as the pressure point | Are housing, wages, hospitals, schools, planning, tax, or infrastructure being reduced to one blamed group? |
| Claim ordinary people are forbidden to speak | Who is actually being platformed, funded, broadcast, or protected? |
| Demand assimilation as proof of loyalty | Who decides what counts as belonging, and is the standard applied evenly? |
| Turn criticism into martyrdom | Is accountability being reframed as censorship? |

Useful source trail:

- ABC revisited Hanson's 1996 maiden speech and its anti-Asian claims: [Pauline Hanson's maiden speech](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2016-09-14/pauline-hanson-maiden-speech-asian-immigration/7645578).
- ABC reported Hanson's 2016 Senate speech warning Australia was at risk of being "swamped by Muslims": [Pauline Hanson calls for Muslim immigration ban](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2016-09-14/one-nation-senator-pauline-hanson-makes-first-speech-to-senate/7845150).

## Example: Russia And "Traditional Values" As Control

Russia shows how culture-war language can be used by an authoritarian state to narrow civil society, redirect resentment, and mark minority identity as foreign contamination. Anti-LGBT laws and "extremism" designations do not fix corruption, inequality, war costs, or institutional decay. They make a vulnerable group carry the symbolic burden of national anxiety.

| Move | Check |
| --- | --- |
| Describe identity as propaganda | Is ordinary public existence being reframed as recruitment? |
| Call minority rights foreign | Who benefits when domestic citizens are treated as outside agents? |
| Expand extremism labels | Can peaceful speech, art, healthcare, or association become punishable? |
| Pair morality with repression | What corruption, war cost, or power consolidation is receiving less attention? |

Useful source trail:

- Human Rights Watch reported on Russia's expanded anti-LGBT "propaganda" restrictions: [Russia: Expanded 'Gay Propaganda' Ban Progresses Toward Law](https://www.hrw.org/news/2022/11/25/russia-expanded-gay-propaganda-ban-progresses-toward-law).
- Human Rights Watch documented the impact of Russia's 2013 "gay propaganda" law on LGBT youth: [No Support](https://www.hrw.org/report/2018/12/12/no-support/russias-gay-propaganda-law-imperils-lgbt-youth).
- Freedom House describes Russia's broader authoritarian system and suppression of opposition: [Russia: Freedom in the World 2025](https://freedomhouse.org/country/russia/freedom-world/2025).

## Example: Myanmar, Facebook, And Anti-Rohingya Hate

Myanmar is a warning about culture-war bait becoming atrocity infrastructure. Online hate against the Rohingya did not merely distract from politics. It helped make a persecuted minority easier to dehumanise, isolate, and attack.

Useful source trail:

- Amnesty International reported that Facebook's systems amplified anti-Rohingya hate before and during atrocities: [Myanmar: Facebook's systems promoted violence against Rohingya](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2022/09/myanmar-facebooks-systems-promoted-violence-against-rohingya-meta-owes-reparations-new-report/).
- The UN Independent Investigative Mechanism for Myanmar published an analytical report on online hate speech targeting the Rohingya: [Publication of IIMM Analytical Reports](https://iimm.un.org/en/publication-iimm-analytical-reports).
- PBS summarised Amnesty's findings on Facebook and anti-Rohingya hate: [Amnesty report finds Facebook amplified hate ahead of Rohingya massacre](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/amnesty-report-finds-facebook-amplified-hate-ahead-of-rohingya-massacre-in-myanmar).

## Example: India And Citizenship As Identity Politics

India shows how law, citizenship, and religious identity can become a culture-war battlefield. The civic question is not whether border and citizenship systems should exist. It is whether equal citizenship is being replaced by a hierarchy of belonging.

Useful source trail:

- Human Rights Watch reported that India's Citizenship Amendment Bill discriminated against Muslims: [India: Citizenship Bill Discriminates Against Muslims](https://www.hrw.org/news/2019/12/11/india-citizenship-bill-discriminates-against-muslims).
- Human Rights Watch documented broader policy and institutional targeting of minorities: [India: Government Policies, Actions Target Minorities](https://www.hrw.org/news/2021/02/19/india-government-policies-actions-target-minorities).
- Council on Foreign Relations provides background on the marginalisation of Indian Muslims: [India's Muslims: An Increasingly Marginalized Population](https://www.cfr.org/backgrounders/india-muslims-marginalized-population-bjp-modi).

## Example: Brazil And "Gender Ideology" Panic

Brazil shows how "protecting children" language can be used to police education, gender, sexuality, and political legitimacy. Some parents will have sincere concerns. The manipulation test is whether the campaign turns curriculum questions into a broad licence to harass teachers, erase minority students, or distract from land, corruption, violence, and environmental policy.

Useful source trail:

- Human Rights Watch documented attempts across Brazil to ban gender and sexuality education under anti-"indoctrination" frames: [I Became Scared, This Was Their Goal](https://www.hrw.org/report/2022/05/12/i-became-scared-was-their-goal/efforts-ban-gender-and-sexuality-education-brazil).
- Human Rights Watch reported on threats to Indigenous rights under Bolsonaro-era policies: [Brazil: Indigenous Rights Under Serious Threat](https://www.hrw.org/news/2022/08/09/brazil-indigenous-rights-under-serious-threat).
- PBS reported on Bolsonaro's first-day measures affecting Indigenous groups, descendants of enslaved people, and LGBT communities: [Brazil's Bolsonaro targets indigenous groups, LGBTQ rights](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/brazils-bolsonaro-targets-indigenous-groups-lgbtq-rights-on-1st-day-as-president).

## Check The Frame

- Is the school story real, exaggerated, or invented?
- Why is this story receiving more attention than the housing decision?
- Who benefits if people stop watching the rent debate?
- Are people being asked to hate a group instead of understand a system?
- Are people being asked to surrender oversight because the leader claims the emergency is too urgent for normal limits?

## Better Habit

You can care about social issues and still ask whether your attention is being redirected.
